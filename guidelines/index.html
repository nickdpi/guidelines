💖 Community Guidelines 💁🏻

Hi! We (Erin, she/her & nickd, he/they) are a [INTRODUCTION GOES HERE - VIEW SOURCE FOR A HOW-TO] household. We [WHAT WE VALUE].

We might be great at creating positive vibes, but this isn’t a “good vibes only” sort of space. At our best we’re vulnerable with one another, we trust one another, and we share in our joys and our frustrations. So, to make sure everybody feels safe and supported, we’ve put together this document. It’s informed by many longtime members of the space, and we’re deeply grateful for everyone’s contributions in making this a safe space for all.

Our expectations

We welcome all forms of participation, of course, and we humbly expect people to:

  • 🤗 Welcome newcomers. If you’re already active here, the absolute best thing you can do is to welcome newcomers, get to know them, and let us know how you feel about them.
  • 💁🏻 Be considerate. Everything you do affects others. Be mindful of the quantity of space that you take up. Be mindful of how much you’re talking relative to another person.
  • 🕵🏻 Mind others’ privacy. Lots of people in this space feel comfortable discussing private matters. Assume the rules of FriendDA apply here. Additionally, don’t post anything that could violate someone’s privacy, and don’t screenshot any text messages or online conversations without getting the consent of everyone involved.
  • 🖐🏽 Don’t enable abuse. Those with reputations for supporting known abusers, or who actively participate in communities that are known to give cover to those with histories of abuse or accusations of abuse,1 may be asked to leave.
  • 🛡 Prioritize everyone’s safety. If you feel uncomfortable with someone being here because of their past behavior, please let us know.
  • 🌏 Be inclusive. We strive to welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities. Avoid language that excludes others. Learn others’ pronouns.
  • 🙅🏻 Don’t harass anyone. If you’re confused about what comprises harassment, take a look at XOXO’s code of conduct.

Resolving issues

If you believe someone is breaking the rules, or if you feel unsafe around anyone for any reason, please text or approach one of us. If you see something that looks like it might be a problem, or if others approach you with an issue, please let us know as well. We’ll do our best to protect everyone’s privacy and safety.

These guidelines apply no matter who you are, and no matter how long you’ve been around. They even apply to us.
